2 Timothy 1:7

Divine Purpose

Unraveling Walls Parenting is my undoing,

Unraveling into God's purpose—

God's Will, God's Love,

Love my body to death, dear Lord.

Cortisol, the sword that chases, Yet I am the vessel, A fragile home for the Divine. How free is God’s will, When I am captive in Christ's love? To walk a path without blueprint in my blood, In a world that criminalizes my very being. Criminalized, caged in concrete and steel, A box my village votes and pays to build, Where grace meets shame, And I am torn between both. A pilgrim seeking what cannot be found, Yet trusting in the unseen. Pilgrim—not the one whose language stirs guilt, But the one who journeys toward a holy place, In a world that rebels against my birth without trying. Walls built for protection Tumble down, Each brick crumbling, Revealing wounds never healed in the Sun, Or in the Son. Walking paradox— Both prisoner and free, Bound to Christ’s love and my child’s care, Yet aching for the freedom I once knew. Parts of me unfold, Leaving behind the unknown, Unseen, unspoken— Raw and real, Like deep scars etched into my skin. I break, Not to shatter, But to make room for something deeper— A surrender that becomes my strength, A surrender that is my only peace. Once strong, these walls now yield, Cracking open to let something new emerge- A strength not my own, A love too vast to contain, Too pure to ignore. I am a vessel, A journey in progress, Carrying more than I ever imagined. God’s love pours in, Overflowing in Abundance, And with each drop, The walls fall, Making room for what is sacred to grow. I surrender my plans, My pride, My ego, The blueprint I’ve spent a lifetime crafting. For in this unraveled state, I find the true design— One written in light, In the sun of His love, In the Son’s sacrifice. So let the walls crumble, Let the bricks fall, For in the brokenness, I am remade. I am not less— But more, Held together by a love That will never fail, A love that transcends all walls, cages, and stigma. For which my Beautiful, Creative, Intelligent, Resourceful Daughter has grown. Leaving me forever grateful for the c-section that left me dead— And dead, I lie still on that emergency room table so many years ago.
